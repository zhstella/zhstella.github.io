Musical Fingerprints

James Baker, David Ge, Melinda Yao, Stella Zhai

Spotify hosts over 100 million tracks on their platform. Our project analyzes a dataset of over 100,000 unique songs across over 100 genres. Each song has its own distinct, quantitative audio features. We chose to focus on five traits in particular—tempo, speechiness, danceability, energy, and loudness.

With these audio traits, we created musical “fingerprints” for each genre in the form of radar charts. We blend the visual with the auditory, combining these geometric representations with playable Spotify embeds, so you can hear how these abstract traits correspond with the actual music.

We hope you enjoy!

Spotify Audio Features Explained

Tempo, speechiness, danceability, energy, and loudness are the five Spotify audio features that shape how a song and genre feels. Select a trait to explore the five tracks with the highest and lowest values of the trait across all genres.

Head-to-head genre comparison

Each bar shows how much Genre A outscores Genre B on a trait — bars pointing right mean A leads, bars pointing left mean B leads. Toggle between Genre identity (vs. all genres) and Popularity shift (what changes when songs get popular). Hover any bar to see individual values, or click Show individual values to pin them.

Top genre fingerprints

Sort genres by one of the five traits or by the combined popularity signal. Each fingerprint layers genre identity with the five traits associated with popularity inside that genre.

Game 1: Guess the song

A Spotify track's audio fingerprint is shown as a radar chart. Listen to the three songs, and pick the song that is depicted in the fingerprint. After your guess, all three fingerprints are revealed so you can compare.

Game 2: Find the similar song

A source song is shown with its audio fingerprint. Two candidates are listed. Listen to all three, then pick whichever candidate's five-trait fingerprint sits closest to the source song. Euclidean distance across all five z-scored traits determines the right answer.

Takeaways

No two genres share the same fingerprint. No two songs within a genre are identical. But zoom out far enough and structure emerges — clusters of sound, shared tendencies, and traits that pull in the same direction across seemingly unrelated styles.
